journal #3

a sneak peek into my job as a phone repair assistant at Wizard Tech.

For a while now, I have worked part-time at a phone repair business, Wizard Tech, in downtown Burlington, VT. I got hired when the business was just starting out -- when I began, it was just the boss and I. While the business is primarily a place that fixes phones, we also fixes computers, tablets, hand-held devices, speakers, and much more. Throughout my time at Wizard Tech, I have had exposure to a variety of devices, tools, software, hardware, and a plethora of problems and solutions related all of them. My boss helped teach me the ins-and-outs of phone repair, and computer repair, with a concentration on mostly Apple hardware. iPhones and Macbooks have become my specialty.

I also help the business by running their eBay operations. Parting out old PCs and broken phones are my primary responsibilities here, where I dissemble the device completely, then estimate its value and list the disassembled parts on eBay. Because of this, it has been handy to start knowing the going rates of common hardware in the repair industry via primarily eBay. Estimating an iPhone 8 motherboard ICloud Locked? Macbook Top Case & Keyboard Grade B+? I can help ya! In addition, through the course of the business, we also come across a lot of random and old devices, which makes this part of the job a lot more thrilling - feels like a treasure hunt digging into a device I've never seen to uncover its inner hardware!
